[Afterword]

Thank you for your hard work, this is Kagi.
Thank you for picking up "Meisou Potage Ramune Flavor" this time.

This time, I tried making a mini-game called "Mokotan In Shita O!?"
It's a simple job of just preventing Ke-ne-sensei from Mokotan In.

The original concept was a crappy game, but just preventing the In was too boring, so I thought "Kagi, give me my money back," and added game elements like scoring and time.
As a result, it turned out to be more of a surreal game than a crappy game...
Making games is difficult. People like Mi~ya-san and yu-ki-san are crazy.
(Or is it simply a problem of concept drift?)

Flash has interactivity, a strength that other video formats don't have,
so I want to study more and pursue embedding interesting direction and tricks into my works.
It might be interesting if a serious movie starts a game under certain conditions.

Well then, see you in the next work~

August 2010
